Privacy Policy

Effective Date: November 2024

Introduction

At Triphosphate Music, your privacy is a top priority. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you visit our website and interact with our services. By accessing our site, you agree to the terms outlined below.

1. Information We Collect

A. Anonymous Usage Data

We collect non-personally identifiable information such as:

  • Browser type and version
  • Device type and operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent on the site
  • Geographic region (general, based on IP address)

This information is collected via analytics tools such as Google Analytics and is used solely for understanding site performance and improving user experience.

B. Voluntarily Provided Information

If you fill out forms (e.g., to book consultations), we may collect:

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Project details or other form-specific information

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information to:

  • Improve website functionality and content based on usage patterns
  • Provide you with requested services, such as consultations or project estimates
  • Analyze marketing campaign performance

3. Sharing of Information

We do not sell, trade, or share your personal information with third parties.

  • Third-Party Tools: We may use analytics tools (e.g., Google Analytics) to collect anonymous usage data.
  • Legal Obligations: Information may be disclosed if required by law, such as compliance with legal requests or preventing fraud.

4. Data Protection

We take appropriate technical and organizational measures to secure your data. This includes encryption of sensitive information and routine security checks.

5. Use of Cookies

We use cookies to enhance your browsing experience:

  • Essential Cookies: Necessary for site functionality
  • Analytics Cookies: Track user behavior to improve site performance

You can adjust your browser settings to refuse cookies or alert you when cookies are being used.

6. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Request access to the information we collect about you
  • Request correction or deletion of personal data
  • Opt-out of analytics tracking by disabling cookies in your browser or using tools like Google's opt-out browser add-on
