Vocal Mixing Guide

A polished vocal mix is the centerpiece of any track, whether you're recording pop, hip-hop, or acoustic ballads.

1. Setting the Foundation: Preparing Your Vocal Track

  • Clean Up with Editing: Remove background noise, pops, or clicks using tools like iZotope RX or basic DAW functions.
  • Comping for Perfection: Combine the best parts of multiple takes into one cohesive vocal track.
Pro Tip: Properly prepped vocals make the mixing stage easier and more effective.

2. The Core Tools of Vocal Mixing

EQ (Equalization)

  • High-pass filter: Remove unnecessary low-end rumble (below 100 Hz)
  • Midrange boost: Gently lift 1-3 kHz for intelligibility
  • Surgical cuts: Eliminate harsh resonances (2-5 kHz)

Compression

  • Use moderate ratio (3:1 or 4:1) to even out performance
  • Aim for 3-6 dB gain reduction for natural sound

Reverb and Delay

  • Short reverb times for pop and modern genres
  • Use delay for rhythmic effects or filling gaps
Pro Tip: Too much reverb can muddy the mix. Use pre-delay settings to keep vocals upfront while still adding ambiance.

3. Advanced Techniques

Parallel Compression

Blend a heavily compressed copy with the original for thickness while maintaining dynamics.

4. Final Touches

  • Use a limiter on the vocal bus to prevent clipping
  • Compare your mix to reference tracks
