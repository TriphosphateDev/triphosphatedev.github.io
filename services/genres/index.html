Genre-Specific Mixing Guide

Every genre presents unique mixing challenges. Learn professional techniques for hip-hop, pop, electronic, and hyperpop to achieve the perfect sound for your style.

Hip-Hop: Drums that Hit Hard

Punchy Kick and Snare

  • Layer kicks for depth, using EQ to give each layer its own space
  • Enhance snare snap with parallel compression or transient shapers

Vocal Presence

  • Keep vocals upfront using wide but balanced midrange EQ boost
  • Add subtle reverb or delay while keeping vocals intimate

Low-End Clarity

  • Use sidechain compression on bass tracks triggered by the kick
  • Apply multiband compression to control muddiness (50â€“100 Hz)

Pop: Polished and Dynamic

Vocal Glow

  • Use bright EQ boost around 10 kHz for added shimmer
  • Automate reverb and delay sends for emotional moments

Layering for Impact

  • Stack synths and instruments with complimentary EQ cuts
  • Apply subtle widening techniques for a polished soundstage

Radio-Ready Compression

  • Use light, transparent compression to smooth dynamics
  • Employ multistage compression for consistent lead vocals

Electronic: Space and Energy

Tight Low-End

  • Use spectrum analyzer to isolate and shape sub frequencies
  • Roll off non-essential bass frequencies to avoid crowding

Reverb and Atmosphere

  • Layer different reverbs for percussion and synths
  • Automate reverb parameters for dynamic transitions

High-Energy Drops

  • Use filter sweeps and risers to build tension
  • Apply creative sidechaining for pumping effects

Hyperpop: Controlled Chaos

Crunchy Vocal Distortion

  • Use soft clipping for harmonic richness
  • Apply hard clipping with careful high-end filtering

Layering Glitches and FX

  • Combine bitcrushers with reverse delays or granular effects
  • Use extreme pitch-shifting with hard-clipped adlibs

Balancing Controlled Distortion

  • Use multiband compression to tame harsh frequencies
  • Center main vocals while allowing FX in the stereo field
